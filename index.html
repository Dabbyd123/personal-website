About Me

Hi, my name is David Brocker and I am a |from New Braunfels, Tx. I began my coding journey about a year and a half ago and completely fell in love with it. I currently work in healthcare, but apart from taking care of my dog Winnie (more of a small horse now) and hanging out with my girlfriend Lauren, most of my free time is spent coding. If I am not coding then I am listening to coding podcasts, researching, taking online courses, etc. I have always been fascinated with the progression of technology and it's affect on our everyday lives, but I never realized that I could actually learn to code and be a part of it until recently.

I graduated from Texas State in 2016 with a major in psychology and web developement was not even on my radar as a potential career path when I was in school. At the time, I thought that if you were in computer science or CIS then you had pretty much been coding since you could walk. But, after talking to a few friends and family members who were in the tech industry, I realized that I was holding an antiquated view. Availability of information on coding and courses with great content have increased exponentially over the past few years. So I took free online classes with Codecademy, bought a couple of courses on Udemy, and started asking a lot of questions.
